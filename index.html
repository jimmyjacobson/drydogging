dry dog·ging

/drī ˈdôɡiNG/

verb, noun

1. The practice of deliberately limiting oneself to charging an electronic device no more than once per day, regardless of battery level or convenience.

"I've been dry dogging my phone for a week now—it's surprisingly liberating to not be tethered to a charger all day."

2. A lifestyle choice reflecting intentional restraint in personal technology use and power consumption habits.

"She's really committed to dry dogging; even when her laptop hits 5%, she waits until morning to plug it in."

Etymology

Early 2020s; from the combination of dry (suggesting abstinence or limitation) and dogging (persisting or following through), coined to describe the growing trend of digital minimalism and intentional device usage constraints.
